## The Living Fort

Galle Fort is unique. It's not a ruin; it's a living city. Built by the Portuguese, fortified by the Dutch, and polished by the British, it's now a melting pot of history and modern boutique luxury.

### Why Visit?

- **Architecture**: Walk through 300-year-old Dutch colonial streets.
- **Shopping**: The best boutique shopping in Sri Lanka (Stick No Bills, Barefoot).
- **Dining**: Incredible cafes and restaurants inside old colonial houses.

## What to Do

### Walk the Ramparts
Go at 5:30 PM. Join the locals flying kites and watching the sunset into the Indian Ocean.

### Visit the Lighthouse
Sri Lanka's oldest light station. Iconic photo spot.

### Shop 'til You Drop
From vintage posters to hand-woven fabrics, the shopping here is world-class.

## Where to Stay

- **Luxury**: Amangalla, Fort Bazaar
- **Boutique**: The Merchant, Prince of Galle
- **Budget**: Rampart View Guesthouse

## Insider Tip

It gets hot during the day. Explore early morning or late afternoon. Midday is for long lunches in air-conditioned cafes.
